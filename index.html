What's the tree ratio in your neighborhood?

We take a look at the latest tree census and neighborhood census. By Rambo Talabong

There are over 600 thousand street trees in New York City. There are over 8 million New Yorkers. Not every neighborhood has the same share of trees.

In this interactive, we look at each neighborhood and how many street trees they have per 100 people.

Check out a neighborhood:

For this visualization, we used the latest population data on neighborhood tabulation areas by the Census Bureau. We also used the New York City census of trees in 2015.

The street tree census of New York City is interesting because it counts only street trees and excludes trees in parks.

With walking as one of the more preferred modes of transportation by New Yorkers and with the increase of extreme-temperature days, street trees have become more important.
